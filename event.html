PAPER PRESENTATION
DESCRPTION

A Paper Presentation is an academic platform that allows individuals to showcase their research, innovative ideas, or technical findings in a structured and professional format. Participants present their work using tools like slides to communicate their concepts effectively. The event fosters intellectual discussions, encourages knowledge sharing, and offers valuable feedback from experts. It typically involves a presentation followed by a Q&A session, where presenters defend their ideas and clarify doubts. Paper presentations help develop critical skills such as public speaking, research analysis, and time management, while also providing recognition and networking opportunities with peers, academicians, and industry professionals.

RULES
  1. The paper must be the participant's original work. Plagiarism will result in disqualification.
  2. Presentations should strictly adhere to the given time limit (usually 10-15 minutes).
  3. Papers should focus on relevant, well-researched topics with clear objectives and methodologies.
  4. Participants must submit an abstract or paper summary before the event for review.
  5. Use of PowerPoint or other visual aids is allowed but must be pre-approved.
  6. Presentations should be in clear, formal language, free of jargon that may confuse the audience.
  7. Participants must properly cite any sources used in their paper to avoid plagiarism.
  8. After the presentation, participants must be ready to answer questions from the judges and audience.
  9. Participants are expected to dress professionally for the presentation.
  10. Adhere to the schedule and be punctual for the event to ensure smooth flow and respect for other presenters.
